poem
Jan 1 - Written By Matt Lombardi

Nü Yír

gaudy vestiges of celebration,
comically discarded,
crushed under passing tires.
the night's emetics
stain the day's sidewalks.
grey languor hangs on the clouds,
leeching off the harried and torn skies.
and a bitter cold nibbles on any flesh left exposed.
last night, I watched impassively.
as the tears and smeared makeup,
of hollowed
women,
ran down the train car floor, pooling at my feet.
until a deluge of saltwater,
opaque with mascara,
burst out of the subway doors at each stop.
this glacial morning,
the stations are empty.
aside from the harried forms of the dispossessed.
forced to bivouac from the cold.
to hyemate under newspapers.
outside, worn faces dot the stoops and fire escapes,
relishing the first inhalation of the year.
their smoke coalesces overhead.
no longer a silky blue-
grey.
but a rose pink.
in the rising sunbeams.
despite the grim physiognomy,
despite the unresolved tensions,
despite the beleaguered bodies
shuffling up and down the avenue,
or pouring out of the subway overhead,
fatigued and remorseful,
returning from a dingy apartment with
solitary regret —
surely, despite it all,
our borough is an optimist.
even now, as the city awakens with renewed vigor,
beauty is alive in the filth,
the glass shattered underfoot is more colorful,
the sodden air more vibrant,
on New Year's Day.
