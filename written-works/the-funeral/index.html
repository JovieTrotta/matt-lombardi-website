poem
Aug 13 - Written By Matt Lombardi

The Funeral

after the funeral.
we walked abstractedly.
tracing arcs through the graveyard's grids.
short bursts of frozen, wintry wind.
threaded their way through the power lines.
crossing, silhouetted overhead.
like so many cracks in the eggshell sky.
I felt a dark pitch hovering over us.
threating to spill.
a canvas of woe.
upon our mournful troupe.
I saw a clenched fist in the distance.
and then I heard the wind whistle your name.
once, twice.
too many times to be imagined.
or hallucinatory.
a voice.
finding these fragile bodies, as one.
all the more distant yet.
in the spaces that opened up between us as we walked.
it was perhaps hopeless.
that eventually this fist — still distant.
would unclench.
that these spaces would become.
solid.
and black.
and vitreous.
and monolithic.
like sheer menhirs of obsidian.
but reassured, we mourned as one.
a body, as one.
but mind, yet solitary.
now.
as one last fragment of frost.
a winter you endured as a child.
fades to warmth.
it fades as memory does.
lost in naïve thought.
the empty space between us once again.
widens and becomes a barrier.
a blockade.
materializes with memories.
petrified.
on the other side of time.
you sink deeper into the earth.
and flowers gnaw at your skin.
while I am torn.
from the cemetery path.
into the sky.
by a frightful force.
of god or demon, it matters not.
