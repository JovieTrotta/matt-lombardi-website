poem
Nov 6 - Written By Matt Lombardi

1999

black, inky symbols.
left-aligned hieratic dots
on thin white leaves.
bleached slices of forest flesh.
nonsense romance;
he speaks (and I vanish).
patterned light strikes the retina.
synthesizing information.
we believe that this is "seeing",
that mediating imagination
is our favorite construct.
carefully now â€“
the symbols start drifting,
skittering around the pages like so many ants.
words begin to garble and bubble,
ink (like tar),
melts off the pages (like feathers),
and falls
to the desk in small droplets.
thick, viscid white pulp pours like glue.
new phonemes are born in the formless black splatter.
unfathomable, emblematic gestures.
letters gather like cells,
forming a new language,
in a liquid book,
on the veneer of an old desk.
a compendium of galaxies gathers in the void of matter.
